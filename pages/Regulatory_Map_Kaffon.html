Tanzania Revenue Authority
Central government tax authority, est. 1995. Administers Corporate Tax, PAYE, SDL, VAT, Withholding Tax, Excise Duty, and other levies.
Employer Duties
Deduct PAYE from employee salaries monthly
Remit to TRA by the 7th of the following month
Pay SDL at 3.5% of gross amounts (employees > 10)
File VAT Return by the 20th
Remit Withholding Tax (applicable payments)
File Corporate Tax (30%) with quarterly provisional installments
Issue EFD Receipts
Final Return within 6 months after financial year ended
Employee Duties
Obtain a TIN (Taxpayer Identification Number)
PAYE is deducted automatically based on progressive brackets
Obtain NIDA
National Social Security Fund

Workers Compensation Fund

Ministry of Labour
Government body overseeing labour standards, employment regulation, and work permits under the Employment and Labour Relations Act (Cap. 366) and the Non-Citizens Employment Regulation Act (NERA) 2015, both amended in March 2025.
Employer Duties
Provide written employment contracts for all employees
Submit returns on employment of non-citizens (Form TFN 903)
Sponsor work permits (A/B/C)
Demonstrate that the role cannot be filled by a Tanzanian national
Comply with minimum wage orders, working hours (9h/day, 45h/week) and leave entitlements
Renew work permits (60 days)
Employee Rights
28 days paid annual leave
84 days paid maternity leave (100 for multiples)
3 days paternity leave
126 days sick leave (63 full pay, 63 half pay)
Overtime at 1.5x basic pay (max 50 overtime hours per 4 weeks)
Access to CMA (Commission for Mediation and Arbitration) for dispute resolution
Foreign employees: Permits required
Immigration Services Department
Government department issuing residence permits for all foreigners intending to stay in Tanzania for more than 90 days. Integrated with the PM Office Labour via OWAIS (Online Work and Immigration System) for combined work/residence permit processing.
Permit Classes
Class A: self-employed foreigners, investors
Class B: expatriates with specific professions employed by a Tanzanian employer
Class C: researchers, retirees, missionaries, volunteers, etc.
Duties
Foreign Employees:
Apply before entering Tanzania (Class B and C)
Submit renewal applications (60 days before expiry)
Carry valid permit at all times
Dependants need separate permits
Employers:
Sponsor work/residence permit
Succession plan (Tanzanian employees)
Report changes
Non-Compliance
Employers engaging non-citizens without valid work permits face substantial fines, imprisonment, or both
BRELA
The Business Registrations and Licensing Agency. Government agency responsible for company registration, business licensing, trademarks and patents in mainland Tanzania. Operates under the Companies Act (Cap. 212).
Company Duties
File annual returns within 28 days after the anniversary of incorporation (with audited financial statements)
Submit and update Beneficial Ownership (BO) declarations
Notify BRELA of any change of particulars within 30 days
All filings via the Online Registration System (ORS)
Consequences of Non-Compliance
Late filing results in penalties
Extended defaults may lead to the company being struck off the register and officers taken to court
Since April 2025, companies without BO information on file cannot perform any transactions with BRELA
Kigamboni Municipal Council
One of five municipal councils forming Dar es Salaam City, established in 2015. Local government authority responsible for issuing Schedule B business licenses, collecting local levies, and providing local services.
Company Duties
Obtain and annually renew a Class B Business License from the Municipal Council
Submit application Form TFN 211 with Certificate of Incorporation, TIN, and proof of premises
Pay local government levies
Comply with local bylaws and municipal regulations
Apply for NIDA (WEN)
Consequences of Non-Compliance
Operating without a valid business license is illegal and attracts fines and/or imprisonment
Ministry of Land
Administers all land in Tanzania under the Land Act (Cap. 113) and Village Land Act. All land is public property vested in the President on behalf of all citizens, so ownership is by leasehold (33, 66, or 99 years).
Process for Foreign Investors
Register investment project with TISEZA
Choose plot
Sign long-term lease (up to 99 years)
Register title with Ministry of Lands
Pay stamp duty (1% of market value) and registration fees (0.2%)
Relevance for Foreign Investors
Foreigners may only acquire land through derivative rights granted by TISEZA (TIC) under the Investment Act
A foreign-majority company is treated as a foreign company and cannot be granted a direct Right of Occupancy
If the investment project fails or the TISEZA certificate is cancelled, land rights revert to TISEZA. Village land must first be converted to General Land by the President before it can be used for investment.
TISEZA
Tanzania Investment and Special Economic Zones Authority. Launched 1 July 2025, merging the former TIC and EPZA. Acts as a one-stop center for investors, centralizing investment facilitation, licensing, and permits.
Company Duties
Registration with TISEZA is mandatory for all investors
Certificate of Incentives: projects min. USD 500k (foreigners), min. USD 100k (Tanzanians)
Report any changes to project (location, shareholders, business type)
Amend the certificate when needed
Incentives
Exemptions from customs duty, VAT and corporate income tax for up to ten years
Duty free importation of essential equipment and construction materials
Exemptions from local government taxes and port charges
Guaranteed transfer of net profits, dividends, and remittance of proceeds
EPZ/SEZ schemes for export-oriented manufacturing, agriculture, trade, tourism, mining, and forestry
Personal Data Protection Commission
Supervisory authority established under the Personal Data Protection Act (PDPA) No. 11 of 2022, in force since May 2023. Monitors compliance, handles complaints, and enforces data protection rules.
Company Duties
Register as data controller
(Register as data processor)
Appoint a Data Protection Officer
Conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) where required
Comply with principles of lawfulness, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy and storage limitation
Report data breaches to PDPC
Obtain permits before transferring personal data outside Tanzania
Individual Rights
For employees, customers
Data subjects can request rectification, blocking or erasure of their personal data
Right to be informed about data collection and processing
Right to lodge complaint with the PDPC
